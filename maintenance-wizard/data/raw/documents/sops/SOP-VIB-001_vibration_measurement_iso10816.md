# SOP - Vibration Measurement (ISO 10816-3)

## Purpose
This [[SOP:SOP-VIB-001]] outlines the procedure for vibration measurement on the Hot Strip Mill (HSM) equipment, following the guidelines set by ISO 10816-3. The purpose of this standard operating procedure (SOP) is to ensure that vibration measurements are taken accurately and consistently, allowing for the early detection of potential faults and the implementation of corrective actions to prevent equipment damage.

## Safety
Before performing vibration measurements, it is essential to ensure that all safety protocols are followed. This includes wearing personal protective equipment (PPE) and following the guidelines outlined in the [[MANUAL]] for the specific [[ASSET]] being measured. Additionally, personnel should be aware of potential [[FAULT]] codes, such as [[FAULT:F2-WRB-001]] and [[FAULT:F3-GBX-002]], which may indicate a need for immediate attention.

## Equipment
The equipment required for vibration measurement includes vibration sensors, data collectors, and analysis software. The [[ASSET]] being measured may include gearboxes, bearings, and other rotating equipment. It is essential to consult the [[MANUAL]] for each [[ASSET]] to ensure that the correct measurement procedures are followed. In the event of a [[FAULT]], the [[PART]] required for repair or replacement should be readily available, such as [[PART:BRG-F2-TRB-01]] or [[PART:GBX-GEAR-SET-01]].

## Procedure
The procedure for vibration measurement involves several steps, including data collection, analysis, and reporting. The vibration velocity RMS values should be measured and compared to the zone boundaries outlined in ISO 10816-3: alert at 1.4 mm/s, action at 2.8 mm/s, and damage onset at 4.5 mm/s. If a [[FAULT]] is detected, the recommended action should be followed, such as performing a lubrication check per [[SOP:SOP-LUB-001]] or planning a bearing replacement per [[SOP:SOP-BRG-001]]. A [[FR]] should be generated to document the fault and the actions taken to address it.

## Zones
The vibration velocity RMS zone boundaries are used to determine the severity of the vibration and the required action. The zones are defined as follows: 
- Alert zone: 1.4 mm/s - 2.8 mm/s
- Action zone: 2.8 mm/s - 4.5 mm/s
- Damage onset zone: above 4.5 mm/s
These zones are used to determine the required action, such as scheduling maintenance or replacing a [[PART]]. By following this [[SOP:SOP-VIB-001]] and using the zone boundaries, personnel can ensure that vibration measurements are taken accurately and that corrective actions are implemented to prevent equipment damage.
