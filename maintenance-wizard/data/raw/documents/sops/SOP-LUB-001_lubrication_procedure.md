# SOP - Lubrication Procedure

## Purpose
The purpose of [[SOP:SOP-LUB-001]] is to outline the standard operating procedure for lubrication of equipment in the Hot Strip Mill. Proper lubrication is essential to prevent equipment failure and ensure smooth operation of the mill. This procedure applies to all equipment that requires lubrication, including those that may trigger [[FAULT:F2-WRB-001]] or [[FAULT:LUB-UNIT-001]] if not properly maintained.

## Safety
Before starting the lubrication procedure, ensure that you are wearing the required personal protective equipment (PPE) and have followed the necessary safety protocols. The lubrication process involves handling of grease and other chemicals, which can be hazardous if not handled properly. In case of any issues with the lubrication unit, refer to the recommended actions for [[FAULT:LUB-UNIT-001]].

## Materials
The following materials are required for the lubrication procedure:
- [[PART:GREASE-EP2-DRUM]] (EP2 lithium-complex grease)
- Lubrication pump and hoses
- Grease guns and fittings
- Rags and cleaning materials

## Procedure
1. Check the equipment to be lubricated and ensure it is in a safe condition to perform the task.
2. Prepare the lubrication pump and hoses, and ensure they are free from any blockages or damage.
3. Apply the recommended amount of [[PART:GREASE-EP2-DRUM]] to the equipment, following the manufacturer's instructions.
4. Use a grease gun to apply the grease to the equipment, ensuring that all moving parts are properly lubricated.
5. Clean up any spills or excess grease using rags and cleaning materials.
6. Record the lubrication activity in the equipment logbook, including the date, time, and amount of grease applied.

## Frequency
The lubrication procedure should be performed at regular intervals, as specified in the equipment maintenance schedule. The frequency of lubrication will depend on the type of equipment and its operating conditions. It is essential to follow the recommended lubrication schedule to prevent equipment failure and ensure optimal performance.
