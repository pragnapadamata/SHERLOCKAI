# SOP - Oil Sampling and Analysis (ISO 4406)

## Purpose
This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) [[SOP:SOP-OIL-001]] outlines the steps for oil sampling and analysis in accordance with ISO 4406, to ensure the cleanliness and quality of oil used in the Hot Strip Mill. The purpose of this procedure is to provide a systematic approach to oil sampling, analysis, and interpretation, enabling maintenance personnel to identify potential issues and take corrective actions to prevent equipment failures, such as those indicated by [[FAULT:F3-GBX-002]].

## Safety
Before performing oil sampling and analysis, personnel must ensure they are aware of the potential hazards associated with the process, including exposure to hot oil, electrical shock, and chemical contamination. Personal protective equipment (PPE) must be worn at all times, and the area must be cleared of any obstacles or tripping hazards.

## Equipment
The equipment required for oil sampling and analysis includes oil sampling kits, laboratory equipment for oil analysis, and vibration monitoring equipment. The oil sampling kits must be specifically designed for the type of oil being sampled, and the laboratory equipment must be calibrated and maintained regularly to ensure accurate results.

## Procedure
The procedure for oil sampling and analysis involves the following steps:
1. Identify the oil sampling points and ensure they are easily accessible.
2. Use the oil sampling kit to collect a representative sample of the oil.
3. Label and store the sample in a clean, dry area.
4. Transport the sample to the laboratory for analysis.
5. Perform the oil analysis, including particle count, water content, and other relevant tests.
6. Record and interpret the results, using ISO 4406 cleanliness coding and oil Fe particle trending.
7. Compare the results to historical data and equipment manufacturer recommendations.
8. Take corrective action if the results indicate a potential issue, such as a rising trend in oil Fe particle count, which could be related to [[FAULT:F3-GBX-002]] or [[FAULT:F3-GBX-001]].

## Interpretation
The results of the oil analysis must be interpreted in the context of the equipment and operating conditions. A rising trend in oil Fe particle count, for example, could indicate wear on the gears, such as those in [[ASSET:HSM-F3-GBX]], and may require further investigation and maintenance, as outlined in the recommended actions for [[FAULT:F3-GBX-002]]. By following this SOP and using the results of the oil analysis to inform maintenance decisions, personnel can help prevent equipment failures and reduce downtime.
